Chicken Satay (Sate Ayam)

Description

Chicken Satay is one of Indonesia’s most iconic street foods, made from marinated chicken skewers grilled over charcoal and served with a rich peanut sauce. The smoky aroma, tender meat, and sweet-salty flavor make it a crowd favorite at any gathering or food stall. It’s simple, flavorful, and truly captures the essence of Indonesian cuisine.

ingredients:

  • 500g chicken breast or thighs, cut into small cubes
  • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water for 10 minutes)
  • For the peanut sauce:
    • 100g roasted peanuts
    • 1 clove garlic
    • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
    • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce
    • ½ cup warm water

Steps:

  1. In a bowl, mix chicken pieces with soy sauce, lime juice, garlic, coriander, salt, and pepper. Marinate for 30 minutes.
  2. Blend the peanuts, garlic, sugar, soy sauce, and water until smooth to make the peanut sauce.
  3. Thread the chicken onto skewers.
  4. Grill over charcoal or pan-fry until golden brown on all sides.
  5. Serve with warm peanut sauce and rice cakes (lontong) on the side.
