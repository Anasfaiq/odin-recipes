Fried Rice Recipes

Description

Fried rice, or nasi goreng, is one of Indonesia’s most famous dishes, loved for its rich flavor and simplicity. It’s made by stir-frying cooked rice with garlic, shallots, chili, soy sauce, and a mix of ingredients like egg, chicken, shrimp, or vegetables. What makes Indonesian fried rice special is its smoky aroma, often achieved by cooking it on high heat in a wok. It’s commonly served with toppings like fried egg, crackers (kerupuk), and sliced cucumber or tomato on the side. More than just a quick meal, nasi goreng represents Indonesia’s creativity in turning simple leftovers into something delicious and comforting.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of cooked rice (preferably cold, leftover rice works best)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 shallots, finely sliced
  • 1 egg
  • 1 tablespoon of sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
  • 1 teaspoon of regular soy sauce
  • 1 small chili (optional, if you want it spicy)
  • 2 tablespoons of oil (for frying)
  • A pinch of salt and pepper
  • Optional add-ons: chopped chicken, shrimp, or vegetables

Steps

  1. Heat about 2 tablespoons of oil in a frying pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and sliced shallots, stir-fry until fragrant and slightly golden.
  3. Crack in one egg, scramble it quickly, and mix it with the garlic and shallots.
  4. Throw in your cold cooked rice, break it apart using the spatula so it’s not clumpy.
  5. Add sweet soy sauce, regular soy sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. You can also toss in chicken, shrimp, or veggies if you want extra flavor.
  6. Keep stir-frying for 3–5 minutes on high heat
  7. Serve it hot with a fried egg on top, plus crackers, cucumber, and tomato slices on the side.
